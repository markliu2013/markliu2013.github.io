project profile #18

Cairns International Airport

Project:
Cairns International Airport Runway 33 Threshold RESA Upgrade
Location:
Cairns, QLD
Client:
Cairns Port Authority
Value:
$1.38m
Construction:
August 2008 to September 2008

Description

This project involved the construction of an extension to the turning node and RESA on International Runway 33, using high-strength pavement. Cairns International Airport at this time was the fifth busiest airport in Australia and had to remain operational throughout the almost two years of reconstruction. For this project, works had to be undertaken while the runway was in full use, with no delays to aircraft.

Works

Key elements in this project included:

  • Extension of runway turning node with additional high strength pavement and shoulder-strength pavement
  • Extension of Runway Emergency Safety Area (RESA) with additional shoulder-strength pavement
  • Extension of RESA beyond the existing southerly limit with a pavement taper
  • Relocation of electrical services around the extended pavements area
  • Drainage

Challenges

  • Working airside, at the end of a live international runway, in controlled high-security area.
  • Acid sulphate soil management

Our Management

The team communicated on a regular basis with all project stakeholders to provide updates on progress as well as planned works in advance.

Value Add

At the conclusion of the five separate contracts awarded to CivilTeam throughout the two year redevelopment of the Cairns International Airport, the client expressed his appreciation of the CivilTeam crew.
